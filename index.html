Privacy Policy

Octasquare Privacy Policy (U.S. Only)
Effective Date: September 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Octasquare ("we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, and protects personal information in the United States. It applies to personal data provided directly to us by individuals or by others acting on their behalf.

Who Is Collecting Data

Octasquare, a Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) consulting firm based in the United States, is the data controller for information collected through our website, forms, communications, or services.

Data We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Contact details: name, email address, phone number, company name.
  • Professional information: job title, role, and industry.
  • Service-related information: details provided in inquiries, assessment forms, or during consulting engagements.
  • Technical data: basic website usage data (e.g., IP address, browser type, date/time of visit).

We do not knowingly collect sensitive personal data (such as health, biometric, or financial account details) unless required for a specific engagement and disclosed at that time.

Processing of Personal Data

We process personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide GRC, compliance, and risk consulting services.
  • To respond to inquiries and communicate with clients.
  • To manage internal operations, recordkeeping, and billing.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations.
  • To improve our website and services.

Storage of Personal Data

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined above, unless a longer retention period is required by law, regulation, or contractual obligation.

Disclosure/Sharing of Personal Data

We do not sell personal data. We may share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With service providers who support our operations (e.g., IT, cloud hosting, email systems).
  • With regulators, auditors, or legal authorities where required by law.
  • As part of a business transaction (e.g., merger or acquisition) with appropriate safeguards.

All third parties are required to protect personal information in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable U.S. laws.

Cross-Border Transfers

Octasquare operates primarily in the United States. Personal information may occasionally be transferred to trusted service providers outside the U.S. if required for IT hosting or secure storage. In such cases, we implement appropriate safeguards to protect your data.

Your Rights

Depending on your state of residence (for example, California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have the right to:

  • Know what personal data we collect, use, and disclose.
  • Request access to or deletion of your personal data.
  • Opt-out of the sale or sharing of your data (we do not sell personal data).
  • Correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Limit the use of sensitive personal data (if ever collected).

To exercise your rights, please contact us using the details below. We will verify your request in accordance with applicable laws.

Data Privacy

Octasquare Data Privacy Statement
Effective Date: September 2025

This Data Privacy Statement outlines Octasquare's commitment to protecting your personal information and ensuring transparent data practices throughout our GRC consulting services.

Data Protection Principles

We adhere to the following core principles in all data processing activities:

  • Lawfulness: We process data only when we have a legal basis to do so
  • Purpose Limitation: Data is collected for specific, legitimate purposes
  • Data Minimization: We collect only what is necessary for our services
  • Accuracy: We keep personal data accurate and up-to-date
  • Storage Limitation: Data is retained only as long as necessary
  • Integrity & Confidentiality: We ensure appropriate security measures

Data Subject Rights

Depending on applicable laws, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access your personal information
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure (deletion) of your data
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to withdraw consent

Data Security Measures

We implement comprehensive security measures including:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Access controls and authentication systems
  • Regular security assessments and audits
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Incident response procedures
  • Secure data disposal processes

Third-Party Data Sharing

We maintain strict controls over third-party data sharing and ensure all partners meet our data protection standards through contractual agreements and due diligence processes.

Code of Conduct

Octasquare Professional Code of Conduct
Effective Date: September 2025

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards and professional behaviors expected of all Octasquare team members, consultants, and partners in our commitment to GRC excellence.

Core Values and Ethics

  • Integrity: We conduct ourselves with honesty, transparency, and moral uprightness in all professional interactions
  • Professional Competence: We maintain and enhance our skills through continuous learning and certification
  • Confidentiality: We protect client information and proprietary data with the highest level of discretion
  • Independence: We maintain objectivity and avoid conflicts of interest in our professional judgments
  • Professional Behavior: We act in a manner that enhances the reputation of our profession

Client Relationships

  • Provide services with professional competence and due care
  • Clearly communicate service scope, limitations, and expected outcomes
  • Respect client confidentiality and protect sensitive information
  • Disclose any potential conflicts of interest promptly
  • Deliver services according to agreed timelines and quality standards
  • Maintain professional boundaries in all client interactions

Professional Standards

  • Adhere to all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards
  • Maintain relevant professional certifications and continuing education
  • Stay current with evolving GRC best practices and methodologies
  • Provide accurate and complete information in all professional communications
  • Accept only assignments for which we are qualified

Workplace Conduct

  • Treat all colleagues, clients, and stakeholders with respect and dignity
  • Foster an inclusive and collaborative work environment
  • Prohibit discrimination, harassment, or retaliation in any form
  • Maintain appropriate professional communications
  • Report violations of this code through appropriate channels

Compliance and Reporting

All team members are expected to:

  • Understand and comply with this Code of Conduct
  • Report potential violations promptly and in good faith
  • Cooperate fully with investigations
  • Seek guidance when facing ethical dilemmas

Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination of employment or consulting agreements.

Professional Disclosure

Octasquare Professional Disclosure Statement
Effective Date: September 2025

This Professional Disclosure Statement provides important information about Octasquare's qualifications, services, limitations, and professional relationships to ensure transparency in our client engagements.

Professional Qualifications

  • Lead Consultant: Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) - ISACA
  • Continuing Education: We maintain required CPE credits and stay current with industry developments
  • Professional Memberships: ISACA, IIA (Institute of Internal Auditors), and other relevant professional organizations
  • Experience: Specialized expertise in governance, risk management, and compliance across multiple industries

Service Scope and Limitations

  • Our services are advisory in nature and do not guarantee specific compliance outcomes
  • Final decisions regarding risk acceptance and compliance strategies remain with client management
  • We recommend consulting legal counsel for specific regulatory interpretations
  • Implementation success may vary based on organizational factors beyond our control
  • Our assessments are based on information provided and observations made during the engagement period

Independence and Objectivity

  • Octasquare maintains independence from technology vendors and solution providers unless explicitly disclosed
  • We do not receive commissions or financial incentives from recommended third-party products or services
  • Any potential conflicts of interest are disclosed prior to engagement
  • Our recommendations are based solely on client needs and industry best practices

Liability and Responsibility

  • Our liability is limited to the scope of services outlined in our engagement agreements
  • Clients retain responsibility for implementing recommendations and maintaining ongoing compliance
  • We carry professional liability insurance appropriate to our service offerings
  • Detailed terms and conditions are specified in individual service agreements

Professional Standards Adherence

  • We adhere to ISACA's Code of Professional Ethics
  • Compliance with IIA International Standards for Professional Practice
  • Ongoing professional development and certification maintenance
  • Quality assurance processes for all deliverables

Third-Party Relationships

We may collaborate with trusted partners for specialized services. All such relationships are disclosed, and partner qualifications are verified to meet our professional standards.
